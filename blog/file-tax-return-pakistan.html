In recent years, the statutory due date for individuals and AOPs has typically been around 30 September after the end of the tax year, with FBR sometimes extending deadlines through official notifications. Always check the latest FBR announcement for the current year instead of relying on a fixed calendar date.

Who Must File Tax Returns?

FBR requires returns from more people than just those above the basic tax slab. In practice, you may be legally required to file a return if you fall in any of these categories:

  • Your annual income is at or above the basic threshold notified by FBR for that year (for recent years, this has been around Rs. 600,000 for many individuals).
  • You own specified assets such as vehicles above a certain engine capacity or immovable property above defined size/value limits.

Step-by-Step Filing Process

Step 1: Register on IRIS Portal

  1. Visit: iris.fbr.gov.pk.
  2. Click "Registration" or "Sign up" if you don't have an account.
  3. Enter your CNIC, mobile number, and email as asked.
  4. Verify OTP codes sent to your phone and email.
  5. Create a secure password and save it safely.

Step 2: Gather Required Information

Having the right data ready makes filing much faster.

  • Salaried individuals: Salary certificate or annual salary slip, record of tax deducted at source.
  • Business owners: Summary of sales/receipts, expenses, and if available, basic profit & loss and balance sheet.
  • Freelancers: Bank statements, export remittance details, and platform statements (e.g., Upwork, Fiverr, Payoneer).
  • Property owners: Rental income details, property ownership documents.
  • Everyone: Bank account details, information on vehicles, investments, and any advance or withholding taxes paid.

Step 3: Login and Start Your Return

  1. Login to IRIS with your CNIC/NTN and password.
  2. Open the Income Tax Return option for the relevant tax year.
  3. Create a new return if no draft exists.
  4. Select your category (salaried, business, or mixed income) as per portal options.

Step 5: Declare Assets, Liabilities & Expenses

For individuals, filing often includes a wealth statement showing what you own and owe.

  • Assets: Properties, plots, vehicles, bank balances, investments, significant cash, valuables.
  • Liabilities: Bank loans, personal loans, credit card balances.
  • Annual household expenses: Approximate yearly spending on living costs, utilities, education, etc.

Step 6: Let IRIS Calculate Your Tax

Based on the slabs and formulas configured for that tax year, IRIS will compute your tax payable or refund position automatically once all required fields are filled.

You can cross-check the result using an external tax calculator (like PakTaxCalc) to make sure numbers look reasonable before you submit.

Step 7: Submit & Save Acknowledgment

  1. Use the preview option to review your full return and wealth statement.
  2. If tax is due, generate a payment challan (PSID) and pay it through your bank's supported channels.
  3. After payment shows in IRIS, click "Submit" to file the return.
  4. Download and save the acknowledgment and filed return PDF for your records.
