Complete Guide to Income Tax in Pakistan 2026

PakTaxCalc Team ‚Ä¢ February 16, 2026

Income tax is a critical part of Pakistan's tax system. Whether you're a salaried individual or self-employed, understanding how income tax works can help you save money and stay compliant with FBR regulations.

What is Income Tax?

Income tax is a percentage of your annual income that you pay to the Federal Board of Revenue (FBR). The tax rate depends on your income slab.

Income Tax Slabs 2026

Here are the latest income tax slabs for tax year 2026:

  • Up to Rs. 600,000: 0% (No tax)
  • Rs. 600,001 to Rs. 1,200,000: 5%
  • Rs. 1,200,001 to Rs. 2,400,000: 15%
  • Rs. 2,400,001 to Rs. 3,600,000: 25%
  • Rs. 3,600,001 to Rs. 6,000,000: 30%
  • Above Rs. 6,000,000: 35%

Example Calculation

Let's say your annual income is Rs. 2,000,000:

  1. First Rs. 600,000: Rs. 0 (tax-free)
  2. Next Rs. 600,000 (600,001 to 1,200,000): Rs. 30,000 (5%)
  3. Remaining Rs. 800,000 (1,200,001 to 2,000,000): Rs. 120,000 (15%)

Total Tax: Rs. 150,000

Tax Deductions and Exemptions

You can reduce your taxable income through:

  • Zakat payments (if eligible)
  • Provident fund contributions
  • Life insurance premiums
  • Investment in approved pension funds

When to File Income Tax Returns

The deadline for filing income tax returns in Pakistan is typically September 30th of each tax year. Missing this deadline can result in penalties.

Tips to Save on Income Tax

  1. Keep proper records of all income and expenses
  2. Claim all eligible deductions (medical, education, etc.)
  3. Invest in tax-saving instruments (pension funds, life insurance)
  4. File on time to avoid penalties
  5. Use our free calculator to plan ahead

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not filing returns even when income is below taxable limit
  • Forgetting to claim eligible deductions
  • Missing filing deadlines
  • Not keeping proper documentation
