What Is PTA Mobile Tax?

PTA mobile tax is a collection of customs duties and other charges that apply when a mobile phone is brought into Pakistan and registered on the local network. The registration process links your device‚Äôs IMEI to your CNIC or passport and confirms that the required duties have been paid.

If a phone is used on Pakistani networks without being properly registered within the allowed time, it can be blocked from making calls, sending messages or using mobile data, although Wi‚ÄëFi may still work.

Factors That Affect PTA Tax in 2026

While official schedules and amounts can change over time, a few core factors usually influence how much tax is due on a particular device:

  • Customs value of the phone: Higher‚Äëpriced phones lead to higher taxes in absolute rupee terms.
  • Type of registration: Phones registered on a Pakistani CNIC often face a different structure from those registered on a foreign passport.
  • Number of devices: There may be practical limits or different treatment for multiple phones brought in by the same person.
  • Applicable duty and tax slabs: Devices can fall into different customs value bands, each with its own combined rate.

How to Register Your Phone with PTA

  1. Find the IMEI: Dial *#06# or check the box and settings to note the correct IMEI numbers.
  2. Open the official portal: Visit the Device Identification Registration and Blocking System at dirbs.pta.gov.pk.
  3. Create or log into an account: Use your CNIC or passport details and a valid email or mobile number.
  4. Enter device and travel details: Add IMEI, your identification number and, where required, arrival information.
  5. View the payable amount: The system will display the calculated duties and taxes for your case.
  6. Pay using supported channels: Complete payment through the listed banking or digital methods and keep the receipt.
  7. Wait for confirmation: After processing, you should receive confirmation that the device has been cleared for use on local networks.

CNIC vs Passport Registration

CNIC Registration

  • Suitable for Pakistani residents with a valid CNIC.
  • Often associated with more favourable treatment in terms of duration and overall cost.
  • Registration is typically intended to be long‚Äëterm for that user.
  • Useful if you regularly use local SIMs and plan to keep the device in Pakistan.

Passport Registration

  • Designed mainly for visitors and non‚Äëresident users.
  • May involve different tax amounts and, in some cases, shorter validity.
  • Better suited to temporary stays and occasional travel.
  • Rules and limits can vary, so always check the latest PTA guidance.

FAQs

Can I register a phone that is not physically in Pakistan?

In most cases, the registration process is designed for devices that are actually brought into Pakistan and used on local networks. Check the latest PTA guidelines for any exceptions or special rules.

What happens if I never complete PTA registration?

A device that is not properly cleared can be restricted from using Pakistani SIM cards, which means no calls, SMS or mobile data on local networks, even though Wi‚ÄëFi may continue to work.

Can I still sell my phone after registering it?

Many people do sell registered phones, but the process can involve transferring usage or explaining the original registration. Buyers often prefer devices with clear documentation and PTA status.
