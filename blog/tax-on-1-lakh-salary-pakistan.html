Tax on 1 Lakh Salary in Pakistan 2026

PakTaxCalc Team • March 4, 2026 • 8 min read

If you are earning Rs. 100,000 per month in Pakistan, you might be wondering exactly how much tax will be deducted from your salary and what your final take-home pay will look like. Whether you have just landed a new job, received a promotion, or are simply trying to budget better, understanding your tax obligations is essential for effective financial planning.

The good news is that a monthly salary of Rs. 1 lakh places you in a relatively favorable tax position under Pakistan's current income tax structure. In this comprehensive guide, we will break down everything you need to know about tax on 1 lakh salary in Pakistan, including monthly deductions, annual calculations, and practical tips to optimize your tax situation.

💡 Quick Answer

For a monthly salary of Rs. 100,000 (Rs. 1.2 million annually), your estimated monthly tax deduction is approximately Rs. 2,500 to Rs. 3,500, depending on your province and available deductions. This leaves you with a take-home salary of roughly Rs. 96,500 to Rs. 97,500 per month.

Understanding Where Rs. 1 Lakh Salary Falls in Tax Slabs

Pakistan's income tax system operates on a progressive slab structure, which means different portions of your income are taxed at different rates. To understand the tax on 100k salary Pakistan residents pay, we first need to look at where this income level sits within the current tax framework.

With a monthly salary of Rs. 100,000, your annual gross income comes to Rs. 1,200,000. According to the 2025-2026 Finance Act, this places you in the second tax slab, where income between Rs. 600,001 and Rs. 1,200,000 is taxed at a rate of 5% on the amount exceeding the basic threshold.

However, it is important to note that you do not pay 5% on your entire salary. Instead, the first Rs. 600,000 of your annual income is typically tax-free (subject to basic conditions), and you only pay tax on the amount above this threshold. This means for your Rs. 1.2 million annual salary, tax applies to approximately Rs. 600,000 at the 5% rate.

Tax Slab Breakdown for Rs. 1 Lakh Monthly Salary

  • ✓ First Rs. 600,000: Tax-free (0% rate)
  • ✓ Rs. 600,001 to Rs. 1,200,000: Taxed at 5%
  • ✓ Taxable amount: Rs. 600,000
  • ✓ Annual tax liability: Rs. 30,000 (before deductions)

Monthly Tax Deduction on 1 Lakh Salary Pakistan

Most salaried employees in Pakistan have their income tax deducted at source through the withholding tax mechanism. This means your employer calculates your estimated annual tax liability, divides it by 12 months, and deducts it from your monthly salary before paying you.

For a monthly salary of Rs. 100,000, the typical monthly tax deduction ranges between Rs. 2,500 and Rs. 3,500. This variation depends on several factors including your province of employment, any applicable tax credits, and whether you have declared additional deductions such as charitable donations or education expenses.

In Punjab, for example, the provincial government has announced various tax relief measures that might slightly reduce your liability compared to other provinces. Similarly, if you are a filer (registered on the Active Taxpayers List), you benefit from lower withholding tax rates on various transactions, though this does not directly affect your salary income tax calculation.

Annual Tax Payable on 100000 Salary Pakistan

When we look at the complete picture on an annual basis, the tax on 100000 salary Pakistan calculations become clearer. With an annual gross income of Rs. 1,200,000, your tax liability is calculated after considering the tax-free threshold and any applicable deductions.

The basic calculation works like this: your taxable income is Rs. 600,000 (Rs. 1,200,000 minus the Rs. 600,000 threshold). At a 5% tax rate, this amounts to Rs. 30,000 annually. However, this is before considering any tax credits or deductions you might be eligible for.

Many taxpayers are unaware that they can reduce their tax liability further by claiming deductions for eligible investments, charitable donations, and education expenses. For instance, if you contribute to an approved pension fund or make donations to registered charities, these can be deducted from your taxable income, potentially reducing your annual tax to around Rs. 25,000 or even lower.

📊 Annual Tax Calculation Summary

  • Annual Gross Income: Rs. 1,200,000
  • Tax-Free Threshold: Rs. 600,000
  • Taxable Income: Rs. 600,000
  • Base Tax (5%): Rs. 30,000
  • Less: Deductions & Credits: Rs. 3,000 - 6,000
  • Final Annual Tax: Rs. 24,000 - 27,000

Your Actual Take-Home Salary Explained

After tax deductions, your take-home salary is what actually lands in your bank account. For a gross salary of Rs. 100,000, understanding your net income helps you budget effectively and plan your expenses.

Based on the calculations above, your monthly take-home pay would typically range between Rs. 96,500 and Rs. 97,500. This assumes a monthly tax deduction of Rs. 2,500 to Rs. 3,500. Remember that this is just income tax; your payslip might also show other deductions like EOBI (Employee's Old-Age Benefits Institution) contributions, which are usually a small percentage of your salary.

When planning your monthly budget, it is wise to use the conservative figure of Rs. 96,500 as your baseline. This ensures you have a small buffer for any variations in tax calculation or additional deductions that might apply in specific months, such as performance bonuses that could temporarily push you into a higher tax bracket.

What Happens If Your Salary Increases?

Career growth is exciting, but it is important to understand how a salary increase affects your tax situation. If your monthly income rises from Rs. 1 lakh to, say, Rs. 150,000, you will move into a different tax scenario entirely.

With an annual income of Rs. 1.8 million (Rs. 150,000 monthly), you enter the third tax slab where income between Rs. 1,200,001 and Rs. 2,400,000 is taxed at 10%. This means while your first Rs. 600,000 remains tax-free and the next Rs. 600,000 is taxed at 5%, the additional Rs. 600,000 above Rs. 1.2 million attracts a 10% tax rate.

The key takeaway is that as your income grows, planning becomes even more crucial. Understanding which tax slab you fall into helps you anticipate your tax liability and explore legitimate ways to optimize your tax burden, such as maximizing deductions and tax credits available under Pakistani law.

🚀 Salary Increase Impact

At Rs. 150,000/month: Your monthly tax could increase to approximately Rs. 7,500 - 9,000, significantly higher than the Rs. 2,500 - 3,500 at Rs. 100,000. This is why tax planning becomes essential as your income grows.

Tips to Optimize Your Tax on 1 Lakh Salary

Even at the Rs. 1 lakh salary level, there are smart strategies you can employ to minimize your tax liability legally. Here are some practical tips:

1. Become a Filer

Registering on the Active Taxpayers List (ATL) offers numerous benefits beyond salary taxation. While it does not reduce your income tax directly, being a filer means lower withholding taxes on banking transactions, vehicle registration, and property matters. This can save you thousands of rupees annually in indirect taxes.

2. Claim All Eligible Deductions

Keep records of any donations to approved charitable organizations, as these can be claimed as tax credits. Similarly, if you are paying for your children's education or supporting elderly parents, explore whether any applicable deductions apply to your situation.

3. Plan Your Investments

Consider investing in tax-advantaged instruments like pension funds or life insurance policies that offer tax credits. While these require upfront contributions, the tax savings combined with long-term wealth building make them worthwhile considerations.

4. File Your Tax Return

Even if your employer deducts tax at source, filing an annual tax return allows you to claim any overpaid taxes and establish yourself as a compliant taxpayer. This is particularly important if you have multiple income sources or claim deductions.
