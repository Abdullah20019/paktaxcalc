ğŸ¯ Who Can Receive Zakat?

The Quran (Surah At-Tawbah 9:60) specifies 8 categories of people eligible to receive Zakat:

1. Al-Fuqara (The Poor)

Those who have almost nothing and are in extreme need.

2. Al-Masakin (The Needy)

Those who have some income but not enough to meet basic needs.

3. Amil-e-Zakat (Zakat Collectors/Administrators)

People appointed to collect and distribute Zakat (can be paid from Zakat funds).

4. Muallaf (New Muslims)

New converts to Islam who need financial support.

5. Riqab (Freeing Slaves)

Helping free slaves (historically); today: helping people in bondage or debt-slavery.

6. Gharimin (Those in Debt)

People overwhelmed by debt who cannot repay (genuine cases, not self-inflicted).

7. Fi Sabilillah (In the Path of Allah)

Those engaged in Islamic causes: students of Islamic knowledge, da'wah workers, mujahideen (context-dependent).

8. Ibnus-Sabil (Travelers in Need)

Travelers stranded without money, even if wealthy back home.

â“ Common Zakat Questions (FAQs)

Q1: Do I pay Zakat on my salary?

No. Zakat is on accumulated wealth, not monthly income. If you save money from salary and it stays above Nisab for one year, then you pay Zakat on savings.

Q2: Do I pay Zakat on my house/car?

No. Your primary residence and personal vehicle are exempt. Zakat is only on liquid assets and investment properties (on rental income, not property value).

Q3: Can I give Zakat to my poor brother/sister?

Yes! Siblings, aunts, uncles, cousins are eligible if genuinely poor/needy. You get extra reward for helping family. Cannot give to parents, children, or spouse.

Q4: Can I pay Zakat to build a mosque?

Scholars differ. Majority say No - mosques are not one of the 8 categories. Better to give to poor people directly. However, some scholars allow under "Fi Sabilillah" (cause of Allah).

Q5: What if I forget to pay Zakat?

It's still due! Zakat is a debt you owe. Calculate for missed years and pay as soon as possible. Repent and make it right.

Q6: Can I deduct Zakat from income tax in Pakistan?

Yes! Zakat paid is 100% deductible. Provide receipts when filing tax return to claim credit.

Q7: Do I pay Zakat on money owed to me (loan given)?

Depends: If borrower is reliable and will repay: Yes, include in Zakat calculation. If loan is unlikely to be recovered: No Zakat until you receive it.
