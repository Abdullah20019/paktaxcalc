Why Engine Capacity and Mode Matter So Much

The current rate-card structure is built around engine-capacity brackets. That means the right question is not usually your model name or what the showroom advertisement called the car. The right question is the engine-capacity bracket shown in the relevant registration documents. Even a small bracket shift can change the amount materially, so this page organizes the experience around brackets from the start.

The second major driver is collection mode. The annual collection schedule and the lump-sum schedule are not small variations on the same number. The lump-sum amounts are much larger and should be treated as a distinct planning case. That is why the mode selector is prominent rather than hidden as an advanced option.

Together, bracket and mode determine the shape of the cost. Once filer status is added, the user has the three inputs that really matter for a quick estimate.
