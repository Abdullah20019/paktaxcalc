How Salary Tax Is Calculated in Pakistan

PakTaxCalc now treats the homepage as a salary tax calculator Pakistan landing page first. If you are an employee trying to understand how much tax should come out of your salary, the process starts with your annual taxable income, not just the deduction shown on one payslip. Your monthly salary is converted into a yearly figure, bonus is added where relevant, and common deductions such as EOBI or employee provident fund can be considered before the taxable amount is matched to the applicable salaried slab.

This annual view matters because salary tax in Pakistan follows progressive slabs. Income is taxed in layers, not at one flat rate on the entire amount. That is why two employees with similar monthly salaries can still see different results once bonus, deductions, or payroll adjustments are included.

Monthly Salary vs Annual Salary

Many people search using monthly salary figures, but the slab structure itself works on annual income. A salary of Rs. 100,000 per month becomes Rs. 1.2 million per year before bonus. Once bonus or other taxable components are added, the final annual number can shift into a different bracket. This is why the calculator shows both annual tax and monthly impact instead of only one figure.

FY 2025-2026 Salary Tax Slab Summary

For standard salaried calculations on this page, annual income up to Rs. 600,000 is generally treated as tax free. Above that point, tax rises progressively under the current salaried slab structure. If you want the full explanation with examples, open our salary tax slabs guide.

Where Filer and Non-Filer Status Fits In

Pure salary tax is mainly about your taxable salary and deductions, but filer status still matters in the wider tax picture. Filing your return and appearing on the ATL can lower withholding tax on many transactions outside payroll. For that broader context, read our filer vs non-filer guide and how to become a filer article.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common tax questions in Pakistan

What is the minimum income to pay tax in Pakistan?

For standard salaried calculations in FY 2025-2026, income up to Rs. 600,000 per year is generally treated as tax free. Exact liability can still depend on payroll treatment and tax profile.

How is salary tax calculated in Pakistan?

Tax is calculated using progressive slabs. Your annual income is divided into brackets, and only the amount falling in each bracket is taxed at that bracket's rate. This is why annual salary matters more than the monthly figure alone.

What is PTA tax on mobile phones?

PTA-related mobile tax can vary by device value, model, and registration method. The safer approach is to verify the live position through IMEI and DIRBS checks instead of relying only on old online lists.

Is this calculator accurate?

It is designed as a practical estimate for standard salaried cases using current slab assumptions and common payroll deductions. If your compensation includes unusual allowances, arrears, or complex adjustments, compare the result with your payroll record or tax adviser.

How do I file income tax return in Pakistan?

File online through FBR IRIS portal. Login with your NTN, select the tax year, fill in your income details, and submit the return. Due dates can change or be extended by FBR, so always confirm the current deadline. Read complete guide ->

What is NTN and how do I get it?

NTN (National Tax Number) is your tax identification number. For many individual taxpayers, it is linked with their CNIC through FBR registration. Step-by-step guide ->

Do freelancers pay tax in Pakistan?

Yes. Freelancers may have income tax obligations depending on income level, source of income, and tax profile. Complete freelancer guide ->

What is the difference between filer and non-filer?

Filer: Person who files annual tax return (appears in Active Taxpayers List). Gets lower withholding tax rates on transactions.
Non-filer: Doesn't file return. Pays double tax on property, vehicles, and banking transactions. Always file to save money!

How much Zakat should I pay in Pakistan?

Zakat is generally 2.5% of qualifying wealth held for one lunar year once Nisab is met. Because Nisab changes with metal prices, it is better to confirm the current threshold before paying. Complete Zakat guide ->

What tax exemptions are available in Pakistan?

Tax treatment of allowances depends on the type of allowance and how your employer structures compensation. Complete exemptions list ->

How to reduce salary tax legally?

Legal tax reduction usually comes from proper salary structuring, eligible tax credits, and filing your annual return correctly. 10 tax-saving tips ->

What is capital gains tax on property in Pakistan?

Tax on profit when selling property: 0% if held 5+ years, 15% if less than 1 year, declining rates for 1-5 years. Non-filers pay double. Complete CGT guide ->

Do I need to pay tax if my company already deducts it?

If your employer deducts correct tax monthly, you may not owe more. But you must still file annual return by Sep 30 to maintain filer status. File to claim refund if excess tax was deducted.

How long does FBR tax refund take?

Refund timelines vary. Processing can depend on return status, supporting data, and whether your bank information is properly linked in IRIS.

What happens if I don't file tax return?

Late filing can increase your tax cost, affect filer status, and create compliance issues. The exact consequence depends on the taxpayer profile, delay, and the rules in force for that year.

Can I deduct Zakat from income tax?

Zakat can affect tax treatment in some filing situations, but the practical treatment depends on records, eligibility, and the return being filed. Check the current rules before relying on a deduction.

What is advance tax and who pays it?

Advance tax is paid quarterly throughout the year by business owners and self-employed persons. Helps spread tax burden instead of paying lump sum at year-end. Dates: March 15, June 15, Sept 15, Dec 15.

How to check filer status online?

Visit the official FBR ATL lookup, enter your CNIC or NTN, and verify whether your status appears as active. Filer status guide ->

Do overseas Pakistanis pay tax in Pakistan?

Generally NO if you're non-resident (living abroad 183+ days/year) and income is earned abroad. But tax applies on Pakistan-sourced income (rental, business, property sale). Dual taxation treaties may apply.
